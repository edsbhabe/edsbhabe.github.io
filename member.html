Kim Namjoon known under his stage name RM and formerly Rap Monster, is a South Korean rapper, songwriter, producer, and composer under Big Hit Entertainment and HYBE Labels. He is best known as the leader and main rapper of the worldwide sensation boy group BTS. RM was born on September 12, 1994 with the name Kim Nam-Joon. At a young age, RM has always been interested in poetry and hip hop music which paved the way for his passion in writing his own lyrics and music. RM continued to pursue his music, becoming an underground rapper and auditioning for different entertainment companies. After a referral from Sleepy, a South Korean Hip Hop artist, RM auditioned and was offered a spot in Big Hit Entertainment. Eventually, RM debuted as the leader of BTS.https://kpopping.com/profiles/idol/RM
Kim Seokjin Kim Seokjin, known under his stage name Jin, is a South Korean singer-songwriter, composer, and producer under Big Hit Entertainment and HYBE Labels. He is best known as one of the vocalists and visuals of the worldwide sensational boy group BTS. Jin was just walking down a street when he was spotted by a casting agent and was invited to audition for Big Hit Entertainment. With a head-turner presence, sweet vocals, and acting education, Jin became a trainee alongside his fellow members. Eventually, Jin debuted with the group and went on to become one of the most famous artists of his generation. Jin’s first official single ”The Astronaut”, released in 2022 became his first solo entry on the Billboard Hot 100 Chart. It was also named one of the best songs of 2022 by Rolling Stone.
Jung Hoseok Jung Hoseok, known under his stage name j-hope, is a South Korean dancer, rapper, singer-songwriter, producer, and composer under Big Hit Entertainment and HYBE Labels. He is best known as one of the main dancers and the rapper of the worldwide sensational boy group BTS. j-hope took dancing classes at Gwangju Music Academy and became a part of an underground dance team called "Neuron" in which he went by the name Smile Hoya. Finding inspiration in G-Dragon and Beenzino, j-hope initially auditioned for JYP Entertainment but ultimately became part of Big Hit Entertainment. Eventually, j-hope debuted as a member of the group BTS; a boy group that went on to grow as a worldwide phenomenon. With passion and ceaseless efforts, j-hope became one of the most sought after artists of his generation. j-hope released his first solo mixtape, "Hope World" in 2018, and became the highest-charting Korean soloist at the time on Billboard 200. j-hope’s single “Chicken Noodle Soup (feat. Becky G)”, released in 2019 became his first solo entry on the Billboard Hot 100 Chart. With the song he became the third South Korean soloist overall to enter the chart. j-hope released his first official solo album, ”Jack In The Box” in 2022. The album debuted on the Billboard 200 Chart and the album’s two main tracks ”MORE” and ”Arson” both debuted on the Billboard Hot 100 Chart. Shortly after the album was released, he also became the first South Korean artist to headline a main stage at a major U.S. music festival ”Lollapalooza”. His second official solo album ‘HOPE ON THE STREET VOL.1’ debuted at no. 5 on the Billboard 200 chart. With this, j-hope became the first South Korean artist to have multiple albums chart in the top 10 of the Billboard 200 chart.
Min Yoongi Min Yoongi, known under his stage name Suga or Agust D, is a South Korean rapper, songwriter, dancer, producer, and composer under Big Hit Entertainment and HYBE Labels. He is best known as the lead rapper of the worldwide sensation boy group BTS. SUGA was born in Daegu, South Korea on March 9, 1993. He made his humble beginnings as an undergound rapper with the stage name Gloss. Eventually, aside from rapping, SUGA also began to produce music at the age of 13. In 2016 he released his first solo mixtape, "Agust D," under the alias Agust D. ARMYs lovingly nickname him as the 'Grumpy Grandpa' of the group, but behind his lethargic and sometimes motionless demeanor, SUGA is considered as the sweetest member. SUGA became the highest-charting South Korean soloist on the Billboard 200 chart with his second solo mixtape ‘D-2’, in 2020. SUGA’s first official solo album, ”D-DAY”, released in 2023 reached the no. 2 spot on the Billboard 200 chart and earned him his first no. 1 on Billboard’s Top Album Sales Chart. The album’s title track ”Haegeum” became his fourth solo entry on the Billboard Hot 100 Chart. On April 26, 2023, Suga began his D-DAY world tour under the name Agust D. The tour ended on August 6 2023. SUGA made history with the tour, becoming the highest-grossing concert tour by a South Korean soloist. According to Touring Data, he earned a total revenue of $57.2 million with 28 sold out shows in the U.S. and Asia.
Park Jimin Park Jimin is a South Korean singer-songwriter, dancer, composer, and producer under Big Hit Entertainment HYBE Labels. He is best known as the lead vocalist and one of the main dancers of the worldwide sensation boy group BTS. Jimin was a top student in the modern dance department at Busan High School when one of his teachers suggested he audition for an entertainment company. Following this, Jimin tried to audition for several companies and eventually earned a spot in Big Hit Entertainment. In 2013, Jimin debuted with BTS and went on to become one of the most well-known and loved artists of his generation. In 2023, Jimin released his first solo album 'FACE' and upon the release of his title track 'Like Crazy,' he became the first Kpop Soloist to hit #1 on the Billboard Hot 100. Jimin’s second solo album ”MUSE”, released in 2024 earned him his second no. 2 entry on the Billboard 200 Chart, after ”FACE” in 2023. The album’s two main tracks ”Smeraldo Garden Marching Band (feat. Loco” and ”Who” became his fifth and sixth solo entry on the Billboard Hot 100 Chart. In August, 2024 ”Who” also became the longest-running no. 1 by a K-pop soloist in Billboard Global 200 chart history.
Kim Taehyung Kim Taehyung, known under his stage name V, is a South Korean singer-songwriter, dancer, composer, producer, and actor under Big Hit Entertainment and HYBE Labels. He is best known as one of the vocalists and visuals of the worldwide sensation boy group BTS. When he was just fifteen years old, V accompanied his friend to audition at Big Hit Entertainment. He was only there to support his friend, but an executive noticed him and encouraged him to audition. Since V had no plans of auditioning, he had to call his parents to ask for permission. He ended up passing the audition and became an official trainee. In 2013, V made his official debut with BTS and went on to become one of the most popular and loved artists of his generation. Besides performing, V also took on acting projects. He debuted as an actor in late 2016 through the historical drama "Hwarang." On September 8, 2023, V made his official solo debut with the mini album "Layover." The album debuted at no. 2 on the Billboard 200 chart. Two songs from the album ”Love Me Again” and the title track ”Slow Dancing” debuted on Billboard’s Hot 100 Chart, ”Slow Dancing” becoming his at the time, third and highest entry on the chart.
Jeon Jungkook Jeon Jungkook, is a South Korean singer-songwriter, dancer, rapper, producer and composer under Big Hit Entertainment. He is best known as the main vocalist, lead dancer, center, and maknae of the worldwide sensational boy group BTS. Jungkook used to dream of becoming a professional badminton player, but the moment he saw G-Dragon perform on television, he was inspired to become an idol. Jungkook auditioned for the show 'Super Star K' and although he did not pass, he still received casting offers from different entertainment companies. After watching RM perform, Jungkook chose to be a part of Big Hit Entertainment. In 2013, Jungkook debuted with the group and went on to become a worlwide phenomenon. Jungkook made his solo debut with single "Seven" on July 14, 2023 featuring American rapper Latto, and released his second solo single, "3D", featuring Jack Harlow, on September 29. His first studio album "Golden" was published on November of the same year featuring title track "Standing Next to You".
